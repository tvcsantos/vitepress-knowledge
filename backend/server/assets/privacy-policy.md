# Privacy Policy for VitePress Knowledge

Last Updated: June 14, 2026

> **Note:** VitePress Knowledge is an open-source, self-hosted project and is not
> operated by any single company or organization. The party that deploys and hosts
> an instance (the "Operator") is the data controller for that instance and is
> responsible for ensuring this policy is accurate for their deployment and
> jurisdiction.

## 1. Introduction

VitePress Knowledge is an AI-powered chat service that integrates with VitePress
documentation websites. This Privacy Policy explains how the service handles
information when you interact with it. Because the service is self-hosted, the
specific data practices are determined by the Operator running the instance you
are using.

## 2. Information Collected

### 2.1 Information You Provide

- Chat prompts and messages you submit to the AI chat interface
- Any additional information you voluntarily include in your interactions

### 2.2 Automatically Collected Information

- IP addresses
- Technical information about how you interact with the chat interface

Note: Requests are processed anonymously. The service does not maintain user
accounts or persistent user identifiers.

## 3. How Information Is Used

Collected information is used to:

- Provide and maintain the AI chat service
- Process and respond to your queries using the configured AI model(s)
- Monitor usage, detect abuse, and address technical issues
- Comply with the Operator's legal obligations

## 4. AI Processing and Third-Party Providers

Chat prompts are forwarded to a [LiteLLM](https://docs.litellm.ai/docs/simple_proxy)
proxy configured by the Operator. LiteLLM in turn routes requests to one or more
Large Language Model (LLM) providers chosen and configured by the Operator (for
example, OpenAI-compatible APIs, hosted model vendors, or self-hosted models).

The identity of these providers depends entirely on the Operator's configuration.
Each provider may have its own privacy policy and terms governing how it processes
data it receives. The Operator is responsible for disclosing which providers their
instance uses.

## 5. Data Storage and Security

The service is open-source and self-hosted. The Operator chooses where the service
runs and where any data (such as the configuration database and uploaded knowledge
files) is stored, and is responsible for implementing appropriate technical and
organizational security measures.

## 6. Your Rights

Because the service processes requests anonymously and does not maintain user
identifiers, it is generally not possible to link requests to an individual or to
fulfill individual data access or deletion requests at the application level.

Depending on your jurisdiction (for example, the GDPR in the EU/EEA), you may have
additional rights regarding your personal data. Such requests should be directed to
the Operator (see [Contact](#10-contact)).

## 7. Legal Basis for Processing

Where required by law, the Operator processes personal information on a lawful
basis, which may include your consent, the Operator's legitimate interests in
operating and improving the service, or compliance with legal obligations. The
applicable basis is determined by the Operator and your jurisdiction.

## 8. International Data Transfers

Data storage locations and any cross-border transfers depend on where the Operator
hosts the service and which AI providers the Operator has configured. Operators are
responsible for ensuring such transfers comply with applicable data-protection laws.

## 9. Children's Privacy

The service does not intentionally collect personal information from any users,
including children; all interactions are processed anonymously. Parents or guardians
with concerns should supervise use and may contact the Operator with questions.

## 10. Contact

This open-source project does not operate any instance and has no central contact.

- For questions about how a specific deployment handles your data, contact the
  Operator of the instance you are using.
- For questions about the open-source project itself (bugs, features, security),
  please use the project's repository issue tracker.

## 11. Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. Material changes will be
reflected here along with an updated "Last Updated" date. Operators should review
this policy whenever they update the service.

## 12. Data Retention

Any personal information is retained only as long as necessary for the purposes
described above, unless a longer retention period is required or permitted by law.
Retention specifics are determined by the Operator's configuration and policies.
